5. Logo and branding recommendations

You asked for recommendations on making this look polished and professional. A logo is the highest-leverage visual investment for a GitHub repo because it shows up in the README hero, the social-preview card, the favicon, and the dashboard header.

Logo concept directions

Three directions, in increasing abstraction. Any of them work; pick the one that reads most naturally to your audience.

Direction A: Network + flask

An abstract Erlenmeyer flask outline with neural-network nodes inside. Visually signals "lab + AI". Risk: feels generic.

Direction B: Three-node graph

Three connected nodes representing skills, sub-agents, and knowledge bases (the paper's three architectural fundamentals), with the working scientist as a fourth orienting node. Visually maps the architecture. Best alignment with the paper.

Direction C: Stylised initials

Letterform "SLA" or "SLAF" as a clean monogram. Lowest-risk option. Easy to scale down to favicon. Works well as a wordmark next to the full name.

My recommendation: Direction B. It encodes the architectural argument in the visual, which makes the logo do double work (brand mark and conceptual cue).

Style and palette

  • Style: minimal flat design. Two colours plus optional accent. No gradients. No 3D. No decorative typography. The repo is academic infrastructure; the logo should read as engineered, not designed.
  • Palette: deep navy (#1c3a5e or #2c5f7c) + warm off-white (#faf7f1) + optional accent (sage green #6d9b6d or amber #d9a55b). The off-white matches the dashboard background; the navy matches the dashboard accent. Continuity across surfaces.
  • Typography for wordmark: Inter, IBM Plex Sans, or a humanist sans. Tracking slightly tightened. Lowercase preferred for modernity; science-lab-AI-framework with the "AI" subtly emphasised (different weight) reads naturally.
