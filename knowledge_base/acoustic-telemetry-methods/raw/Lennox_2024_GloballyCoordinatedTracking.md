# Source extraction: Lennox et al. 2024, Ecography — Globally coordinated acoustic aquatic animal tracking

## Verbatim abstract (partial, from WebSearch)

> "Acoustic telemetry is a popular approach used to track many different aquatic animal
> taxa in marine and freshwater systems. However, information derived from focal studies
> is typically resource- and geography-limited by the extent and placement of acoustic
> receivers. Animals tagged and tracked in one region or study may be detected
> unexpectedly at distant locations by other researchers using compatible equipment, who
> ideally share that information. Synergies through national and global acoustic tracking
> networks are facilitating significant discoveries and unexpected observations that
> yield novel insight into the movement ecology and habitat use [of aquatic animals]."

## Why this paper matters for the framework

framing that underpins the Griffin Lab's telemetry workstream. Specifically it is the
foundational citation for:

- The premise that cooperative networks (FACT, iTAG, OTN, ACT, MATOS) enable detections
  beyond any single study's array

  data sharing
- The ecological importance of long-distance, infrequent movements that focal studies
  cannot capture
