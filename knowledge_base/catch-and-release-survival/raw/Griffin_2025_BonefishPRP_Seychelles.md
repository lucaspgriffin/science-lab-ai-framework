# Source extraction: Griffin et al. 2025, Fisheries Research — Site-specific post-release predation of bonefish in the Seychelles

## Verbatim abstract

> "Bonefish (Albula spp.) support recreational catch-and-release (C&R) fisheries in
> tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to conservation efforts and advancing
> Blue Economy initiatives. However, post-release predation (PRP) poses challenges to the
> sustainability of these fisheries, particularly in predator-dense environments. This
> study assessed physical injury, reflex impairment, and PRP for bonefish (Albula
> glossodonta) in the Alphonse Group of islands, Republic of Seychelles, a
> well-established sport fishing destination. There was a significant effect of air
> exposure on reflex impairment, with fish exposed to air for 30 seconds being more
> impaired than those exposed to 0 or 10 seconds. However, air exposure, among other
> angling event characteristics, did not affect PRP (overall PRP = 13 %). Notably, PRP was
> highly site-specific, with 75 % of predation events occurring at a single location
> where bonefish were 15 times more likely to be predated compared to other sites. Cryptic
> predation was prevalent, as only 17 % of predation events were preceded by observing
> potential predators. Sicklefin lemon sharks (Negaprion acutidens) were responsible for
> most PRP, often tracking and preying on bonefish within an average of 9 minutes after
> release (30–1080 seconds; 545 ± 315 seconds). To reduce PRP risk, these findings
> highlight the need for regular PRP assessments, adaptive management, and site-specific
> strategies that include voluntary actions of anglers and guides to avoid locations where
> PRP is prevalent. Incorporating evidence-informed practices into conservation and
> management plans can promote sustainable recreational fisheries while supporting
> conservation and economic growth in a Blue Economy." (Abstract, p. 1)

## Verbatim quantitative claims with page references

### Headline results

- **Overall PRP: 13%** (Abstract; Methods §2)
- **Site-specific concentration: 75% of predation events occurred at a single location**
  (Abstract)
- **15x higher predation rate at the focal site vs other sites** (Abstract)
- **Cryptic predation: only 17% of predation events preceded by observing potential
  predators** (Abstract)
- **Primary predator: Sicklefin lemon shark (Negaprion acutidens)** (Abstract)
- **Mean time to predation: 9 minutes; range 30–1080 s; mean 545 ± 315 s** (Abstract)
- **Air exposure (30 s) significantly increased reflex impairment vs 0 or 10 s, but did
  NOT affect PRP** (Abstract)

### Study system

- **Site:** Alphonse Group (7.10°S, 52.75°E), Outer Islands of Republic of Seychelles
  (§2.1, p. 2)
- **Atoll details:** Alphonse Atoll: 174 ha land mass, 540 ha lagoon, 402 ha peripheral
  reef flats; St. François Atoll: 2 ha (Bijoutier) + 17 ha (St. François) land,
  encircled by reef before reaching > 2000 m depth, 3732 ha shallow reef flats, 1650 ha
  inner lagoon < 10 m depth (§2.1, p. 2)
- **Substrate:** sand, seagrass, coral rubble; tidal range ~2 m (§2.1, p. 2)
- **Predators:** giant trevally, sicklefin lemon sharks (Negaprion acutidens), great
  barracuda (§2.1, p. 2)

### Field sampling

- **Sampling windows:** January 8-18, 2019, and January 24 to March 12, 2022 (§2.2, p. 2)
- **Gear:** 7-10 wt fly rods, 10-20 lb leaders, size 6-8 barbless flies (§2.2, p. 2)
- **Water depth:** consistent at 0.2-0.6 m (§2.2, p. 2)
- **Capture location classification:** near (< 200 m) or far (> 200 m) from deeper
  water (§2.2, p. 2)
- **Air-exposure treatments:** 0, 10, or 30 seconds (§2.2, p. 2)
- **Reflex impairment battery:** righting reflex, tail grab, eye roll (VOR), body flex,
  head complex; each scored 0/1; reflexes weighted equally 0.2 each; full score 1
  = all reflexes passed (§2.2, p. 2-3)
- **PRP tracking method:** small brightly-colored float attached via small hook in dorsal
  musculature behind dorsal fin; float connected to line ~10 m above fish on 5.4 kg
  test fluorocarbon, spinning rod with bail open (§2.2, p. 3)
- **Tracking duration:** up to 20 minutes post-release (§2.2, p. 3)
- **IACUC:** UMass Amherst protocol 3545 (§2.2, p. 3)

### Economic and conservation framing

- **Flats species annual economic impact in Belize, The Bahamas, and the Florida Keys:**
  exceeds USD 689 million (Smith et al. 2022) (§1, p. 2)
- **IUCN status:** A. glossodonta = Near Threatened; A. vulpes = Vulnerable
  (Adams et al. 2012a, 2012b) (§1, p. 2)

### Cited comparison studies

- **Danylchuk et al. 2007c (Bahamas):** bonefish that lost equilibrium following capture
  were 6× more likely to be predated within the first 20 minutes post-release (§1, p. 2)
- **Lennox et al. 2017 (Anaa Atoll, French Polynesia, blackfin reef sharks):** bonefish
  released without air exposure experienced PRP of 33%, while those exposed to air had
  PRP > 60% (§1, p. 2)
- **Moxham et al. 2019 (Seychelles, surgically tagged bonefish):** PRP ≥ 43% but with
  ~11 minute average hooking-to-release time (confounded for C&R comparison) (§1, p. 2)
